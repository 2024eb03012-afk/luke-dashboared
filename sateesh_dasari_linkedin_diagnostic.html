P1 — Week 1 · Day 3–4 Stop all reposts — write reaction posts instead
When sharing external content (news article, award, product launch), write your own 400-word original post. Structure: "Here's what [company/news] means for India's enterprise AI market:" → cite 1 line from the source → 3 paragraphs of your own expert take → question CTA. This preserves the news hook while keeping content fully original for 360Brew. Immediately delete any queued reposts.
P1 — Week 1–2 Define 4 content pillars and build a 4-week calendar
Pillars: (1) Agentic AI for enterprise — frameworks, common failures, use cases. (2) Sovereign AI & data privacy in India — policy, tech architecture, government initiatives. (3) Deep-tech startup building — honest founder stories, lessons from Continuum AI's first 18 months. (4) Enterprise digital transformation leadership — lessons from 30 years across Oracle, Deutsche Bank, Rolta. Post 3x/week: Monday = educational/framework, Wednesday = story/behind-scenes, Friday = opinion/contrarian. Write every post idea against a pillar before drafting.
P2 — Week 2–3 Publish first carousel: "5 reasons enterprise AI fails"
7-slide carousel. Slide 1: bold hook — "5 reasons enterprise AI projects fail (and how agentic AI fixes them)." Slides 2–6: one problem per slide with a 2-line Continuum AI solution — e.g., "Problem: LLM wrappers collapse under production load. Fix: MidasAI eliminates prompt-reliant architecture entirely." Slide 7: "DM me 'AI AUDIT' for a readiness review." This simultaneously demonstrates product authority, drives saves (360Brew's highest ranking signal), and activates the 4x carousel reach multiplier. Design with Canva using brand colours.
P2 — Week 3 Publish a deep founder story post
The "Today, I took a bold step into the future" post was the highest-performing visible post (61 reactions, 31 comments). That format — honest, personal, milestone-driven — is what resonates most with this audience. Write a follow-up: "What I didn't expect in month 1 of Continuum AI's go-to-market." Hook: "I built enterprise software for 30 years. Nothing prepared me for this." → 3 specific lessons from early GTM → invitation to connect with founders in similar situations. Personal stories are confirmed as the best-performing hook type in the Q4 2025 algorithm report.
P2 — Week 3–4 (ongoing) Start daily commenting — 5 niche posts per day
Target accounts: enterprise AI founders, sovereign tech advocates, India startup ecosystem creators, digital transformation consultants. Comment with genuine insight — minimum 3 sentences that add a new perspective, not just agreement. The Q4 2025 report confirms top 1% creators average 150–165 comments per week. Set a 15-minute calendar block each morning. Build a list of 50 target creators in your niche to engage with consistently — this is the fastest follower growth lever that requires zero content creation.
P3 — Month 2 Build content tracking system + 90-day BOFU sequence
Create a Google Sheet with columns: post date, format (text/carousel/image), hook type, pillar, impressions, comments, saves, DMs generated. Review every Friday — 15 minutes. This builds the conversion layer systematically without feeling salesy because it follows 6–8 weeks of trust-building TOFU/MOFU content.
P3 — Month 2–3 Repurpose top posts into multi-format assets
Once a content rhythm is established, repurpose top-performing posts: turn the "5 reasons enterprise AI fails" carousel into a LinkedIn PDF guide with a lead-gen CTA. Convert 3 framework posts into a newsletter issue. Record a 60-second video version of the best story post. Per the Donnelly checklist Section 7 (Multiformat Repurposing), every top post should yield 3+ additional formats. This multiplies reach without requiring new ideas.
